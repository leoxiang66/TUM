Motivation

  • Basic autoregressive models and Markov Chains are very restrictive/simple
    • Do not well capture complex, real-world data
  • Next: Probabilistic latent variable models for sequences of observations X1,X2,…,XTX_{1}, X_{2}, \ldots, X_{T}X1​,X2​,…,XT​
    • Enable to capture more complex behavior
    • time-step: discrete
    • observations: discrete/continuous

example
  • Object-tracking:
    • Xt=X_{t}=Xt​= location of a moving object at time-step t
  • Time-series forecasting:
    • Xt=X_{t}=Xt​= measurement of a sensor at time-step t\mathrm{t}t (weather, stock market, ...)
  • Natural language processing:
    • Xt=tX_{t}=tXt​=t-th word in a sentence

Motivation 1
在很多实际运用中, Markov Property并不实际
→ observationsXtX_{t}Xt​不仅仅取决于前面的几项
→需要考虑long-term dependencies,同时要使模型的参数数量较小

Motivation 2
在很多实际运用中, states并不能直接观测到, i.e. observations < states

e.g. tracking location of an airplane:
  • observations: noisy measurements of airplane locations XtX_{t}Xt​
  • states: physical vector quantities (position, velocity, accelaration...) ZtZ_{t}Zt​


—> 引入hidden/latent states ZtZ_{t}Zt​, 使这些隐状态满足Markov Property, 但是observations不需要有Markov Property

Definition of HMM

A HMM由两个sequences组成:
  • a sequence of hidden/latent random variables [Z1,…,ZT]\left[Z_{1}, \ldots, Z_{T}\right][Z1​,…,ZT​]
  • a sequence of observed random variables [X1,…,XT]\left[X_{1}, \ldots, X_{T}\right][X1​,…,XT​]

such that: (HMM假设)
  1. Z1,…,ZTZ_{1}, \ldots, Z_{T}Z1​,…,ZT​ satisfy the Markov property: 任意时刻的状态 (latent variables)只取决于前一时刻的状态
    transition probabilities:
    P(Zt+1∣Zt,Zt−1,…,Z1)=P(Zt+1∣Zt)\mathrm{P}\left(Z_{t+1} \mid Z_{t}, Z_{t-1}, \ldots, Z_{1}\right)=\mathrm{P}\left(Z_{t+1} \mid Z_{t}\right)P(Zt+1​∣Zt​,Zt−1​,…,Z1​)=P(Zt+1​∣Zt​)
  2. XtX_{t}Xt​ depends only on ZtZ_{t}Zt​ : 任意时刻的观测仅由该时刻的状态决定 (先有状态,才有观测)
    emission probabilities:
    P(Xt+1∣Z1,…,ZT,X1,…,XT,)=P(Xt+1∣Zt+1)\mathrm{P}\left(X_{t+1} \mid Z_{1}, \ldots, Z_{T}, X_{1}, \ldots, X_{T,}\right)=\mathrm{P}\left(X_{t+1} \mid Z_{t+1}\right)P(Xt+1​∣Z1​,…,ZT​,X1​,…,XT,​)=P(Xt+1​∣Zt+1​)
(3). conventions:
  • time-steps: discrete t∈{1,2,…,T}t \in\{1,2, \ldots, T\}t∈{1,2,…,T}
  • latent random variables: Zt∈{1,2,…,K}Z_{t} \in\{1,2, \ldots, K\}Zt​∈{1,2,…,K}
  • observations: discrete/continuous
